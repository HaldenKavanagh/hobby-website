The Walstead Method

How it works Materials

The walstead method is a natural way to set up your fishtank, without the need for water changes, regular cleaning, 02, C02, and eventually food. Aside from the cost of the tank, it can be done for free and requires little to no maintnence.

How does it work?

The most important part of a successfull Walstead tank is the bacteria, without it the tank would'nt function. It is because of this that the substrate is so important. By having a thin layer of soil at the bottom, and a deep layer of sand or gravel above it (atleast 2 inches), we can mimic the natural world to create a truly self sufficient ecosystem.

To understand this, you must know how the nitrogen cycle works.

The Nitrogen cycle and anaerobic denitrification

  • The Nitrogen cycle

    The Nitrogen cycle is basically the food chain for plants and animals in your tank. When things like leftover fish food, dead organisms, and waste products decompose they turn to ammonia. Ammonia is very toxic to your tak, and if it builds up, everything will die. The ammonia is then broken down by nitrosomas (ammonia eating bacteria) It turns into something less harmfull but still detremental to your tank, nitrites These nitrates are then broken down by by bacteria in the genus Nitrobacter into nitrates.

    At this point, a water change would be done to rid your tank of the nitrates, however this is not neccesarily needed. Thats where the walstead method comes in.

  • Anaerobic Nitrification

    Denitrification refers to the process where specific types of bacteria break down nitrates and nitrites into nitrogen gas. Denitrification only occurs anaerobically, meaning it only takes place where no oxygen is present, and the bacteria that performs this is anaerobic, meaning they will die in contact with oxygen

    This is where the Walstead method's minimum two inches of sand comes to play. With a deep layer of sand, a portion of your tank exists where oxygen can penetrate, and thus creating an environment for denitrifying bacteria to live and complete the nitrogen cycle completely. This eliminates the need for water changes. It is very hard to get tis balance perfect, so expect to do mabie two 25% water changes a year
